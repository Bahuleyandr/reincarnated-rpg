# The Atlas of the Five Spokes

Five racial homelands radiate from a central estuary metropolis. The
genre defaults are inverted: the elves are not wise, the dwarves are
not stout, the halflings are not peaceful, the orcs are not violent,
and the humans — far from being adaptable jacks-of-all-trades — are
brittle specialists who die young.

Each spoke has two named towns between Caelum and the homeland city.
Every location ships a regional resource into the central registry
at `content/items/resources.json`.

## Caelum-by-the-Wash (the metropolis)

Where the five rivers meet. The Spoke Square at its heart has a
bronze compass-rose with the inscription in five scripts. Built
quarter by quarter — each race has a quarter that originally housed
only its own people, but most are mixed now. The Wash itself moves
cargo between every spoke and the sea.

**Local goods**: estuary pearl; pidgin paper; everything that comes
through anyway.

## North spoke — the Dwarves

Dwarves are tall, slender, agoraphobic toward enclosed spaces, and
live above the cloudline tending terraced orchards. They make tools
from wood and bone, refuse to enter caves, and consider mining
deviant. Their drink is cider.

- **Three Notches** — the pass town. A switchback up to the
  cloudline begins here. Bow once at each of the three notches on
  the way up. The half-dwarven family who runs the inn has a
  north-open wing for dwarves and a south-walled wing for everyone
  else. *Goods: switchback quartz, mule-tail bone, cloudtea leaf.*
- **Coldspoon** — apothecary town. Cloudtea drying racks and the
  cloud-bitters tincture room. The Hush-readers of the Long Indices
  buy cloud-bitters by the case. *Goods: cloudtea leaf,
  cloud-bitters, summit amber.*
- **Highfield Ascending** — terraced orchard city above the
  cloud-mass. Whitestar apples, Greybark pears, Whitebark cider.
  The Gust-watchers read the wind and approve every planting.
  Sub-populations: **Topgrove** (orchardists), **Skybeam**
  (kite-pollinators), **Whitebark** (cider-makers), **Gust-watchers**
  (weather).

## East spoke — the Elves

Elves live a normal 60-70 years. They are commercial, conniving,
and run banking houses + information brokerages. The "ethereal
serene" mannerism — *the act* — is calculated. Drop in on an elf at
home and meet a different person.

- **Mudmoth** — first mudflat hamlet. Bioluminescent salt-flies lay
  eggs in the mud at low tide; the eggs become a marker dye in
  Saltgale's mudglass kilns. *Goods: glow-fly egg, estuary mud,
  saltgrass.*
- **Tallowfen** — chest-high tallow-reeds in a brackish wetland.
  Rendered into clean-burning candle wax used in elven counting
  houses. *Goods: tallow reed, tallow wax, fen bog-iron.*
- **Saltgale** — pontoon city on the mudflats. Banking pontoons,
  contract-rooms, mudglass kilns at low tide. The act is at full
  performance in the public street; through any window you can hear
  it dropped. Sub-populations: **Branchmen** (banking),
  **Clearseers** (information brokers), **Salt-folk** (wholesale —
  the only elves who never bother with the act), **Vellum** (contract
  scribes).

## West spoke — the Orcs

Orcs are quiet philosophers. They run the only universities. They
write more than they speak. Violence to them is a category error.
Most orcish dinners include long stretches of writing in notebooks.

- **Cataract Mile** — paper-mill town at a mile of cataracts. Workers
  communicate in hand-signs over the constant water-roar. Supplies
  the Long Indices with all its paper. *Goods: river reed, reed
  paper, mill-wood oak.*
- **Quietmile** — the silent town. Under a centuries-old vow of
  silence dating from a plague. The vow-poppy fields ring the town
  and ship petals to the orcish bookbinders. *Goods: vow poppy,
  vow-petal silk, ink onyx.*
- **The Long Indices** — nested library quadrangles built into the
  high desert plateau. Each successive square is smaller and
  quieter; the innermost is the Hush Room, where communication is by
  marginalia. Sub-populations: **Shelvers** (preservation),
  **Cataloguers** (indexing), **Marginals** (commentary),
  **Hush-readers** (silent reading-room caste; vow taken at 19).

## SE spoke — the Humans

Humans are brittle specialists. Each is born into a single craft
they cannot escape. Apprenticeship at 7. Master at 21. Teacher at
35. Dead at 45. Their craft dies with them.

- **Furrowmouth** — single-grain market town. Wheat Day, Barley
  Day, Rye Day. Each day operates only in that grain. Elven Salt-folk
  wholesalers know which day is which. *Goods: furrow grain, wheat
  flour, barley malt, rye meal.*
- **Knot's Landing** — rope-makers' town. A two-hundred-yard
  ropewalk where rope-makers walk backward as the rope twists. The
  town's namesake is a single iconic knot every local can tie
  blindfolded. *Goods: river flax, saltcord rope.*
- **Threadwarden** — inland river-basin city of single-craft
  compounds. The Loom Square, the Cold Forges, the Page Quarter, the
  Furrow Banks (the public commons all four crafts share).
  Sub-populations: **Threadwardens** (weavers; name their cloth like
  people), **Coldhammers** (smiths who hammer iron at room
  temperature — only works in Threadwarden's humid air),
  **Furrowfolk** (single-grain farmers), **Pageborn** (scribes; the
  only humans who learn another tongue, always orcish).

## SW spoke — the Halflings

Halflings are wiry naval mercenaries. They live offshore on coral
reefs in pontoon dwellings, eat once a day at dawn, and run the
only standing navy on the coast. They cut dead coral only; cutting
living coral is a capital offense.

- **Briny Bell** — tide-locked bell-tower town. The bells ring on
  the tide rising and falling, twice a day, on time. Last stop
  before the tidal causeway out to the reef. *Goods: bell bronze,
  tidewater pearl, salt block.*
- **Crab-by-Crab** — fishing port whose entire export is crabs.
  Every species named, identifiable by claw-angle alone. Halfling
  Salt-keepers visit weekly to provision the dawn-meals. *Goods:
  green-claw crab, blue-back crab, sand-runner crab, broth bay-leaf.*
- **The Coral Anchorage** — pontoon city anchored to dead coral
  miles offshore. Six ships in the standing navy; many freelance
  privateers come and go. The Coral Anchor is the four-ribbon
  flagship. Sub-populations: **Tidehands** (regular navy),
  **Reef-cutters** (privateers), **Salt-keepers** (provisioners),
  **Drowncallers** (search-and-rescue and wreckers — the line is
  whose flag the ship was flying).

## Resource registry

`content/items/resources.json` now carries 67 entries (was 20),
each tagged by region via `sourceLocations`. The expansion adds:

- **Regional gems**: estuary pearl, tidewater pearl, summit amber,
  switchback quartz, ink onyx, reef iridium, mudglass prism.
- **Regional ores**: cloudtin (Highfield), fen bog-iron (Tallowfen),
  bell bronze (Briny Bell), loom iron (Threadwarden),
  coralwhite aragonite (Anchorage).
- **Regional plants**: cloudtea, vow poppy, inkwort, saltgrass,
  tallow reed, river flax, river reed, coral kelp, broth bay-leaf,
  Whitestar apple, Greybark pear.
- **Regional crafted goods**: Whitebark cider, mudglass prism,
  Saltgale vellum, reed paper, Marginal folio, named cloth,
  cold-forged blade, vow-petal silk, saltcord rope, cloud-bitters,
  drowncaller flag cloth.
- **Regional foods**: salt-cured fish, three crab species,
  wheat flour, barley malt, rye meal.

Each new location's `availableResources` field reaches into this
registry; the registry's `sourceLocations` field reaches back. The
graph is bi-directional and content-validator-checked.
